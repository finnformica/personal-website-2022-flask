Fuzzy Logic Control

My third year independent research project (IRP) was involved in the traction control subsystem of an 800V electric vehicle. The research involved modelling the EV powertrain using Simulink, and then designing a controller to modulate the output torque of the electric motor. Fuzzy Logic Control is a popular method to reduce torque ripples within the motor; however, the implementation of FLC can be complex as it involves programming the controller behaviour using rules. The graphs below show that FLC was successful in reducing torque ripples and decreasing the settling time of the system.
